Description

After collecting feedback from users, the product team suggested several changes to the menu page of the company app. The UX and UI designers developed a new menu interface that shows the most popular items at the top of the page. You, as an analyst, need to investigate a possible increase in the average order value (AOV) with a new app design. In other words, you need to conduct the A/B test.

The product manager calculated the traffic size for the experiment and suggested the sample sizes. Your task is to check whether a sample with the specified size can adequately reflect the outcome before committing any design changes. Take two samples of a particular size and assess whether they contain significant statistical differences. In other words, conduct the so-called A/A test to check that the parameters, i.e. the sample sizes, of the experiment will produce a statistically fair result.

Objectives

Download a file with the dataset and save it to your working directory. Check if the mean order value is equal in both samples. For this, you need to do the following:

  • Treating the order value as normal-distributed, conduct a hypothesis test to check the assumption for the equality of variances. Apply Levene's test. Print the test statistic rounded to the third decimal place and p-value. Make a conclusion of the null hypothesis (Reject null hypothesis: yes or no) and variances (Variances are equal: yes or no).
  • Based on the results of Levene's test, apply the t-test to the independent samples with equal/unequal variances. Show the test statistic rounded to the third decimal place and p-value, conclude the null hypothesis ( Reject null hypothesis: yes or no) and whether the group means are equal or not (Means are equal: yes or no).

You may want to use implementations of Levene's test and t-test from SciPy. Based on the result of Levene's test set equal_var in t-test. Other parameters remain default.
