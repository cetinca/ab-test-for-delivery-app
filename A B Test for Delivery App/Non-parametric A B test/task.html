Description

In this stage, you need to test a statistical hypothesis and establish whether the average order value (AOV) has changed after switching to the new design. As you may have noticed earlier, the order value is not distributed normally. Let's try to apply a non-parametric test and interpret the results. Since non-parametric tests require fewer observations, the sample prepared in the previous stage is suitable for hypothesis testing in this stage.

What is the best way to present the A/B testing results to the members of your team who are not familiar with the basics of statistical inference? Is the interface in the experimental group better than in the control one AOV-wise? How sure are you about the results? Share your thoughts in the comments!

Objectives

Use the dataset with removed outliers from the previous stage. Test the hypothesis with Mann-Whitney U test and print:

  • The test statistic for the control group (U1).
  • The p-value.
  • The conclusion on the null hypothesis (Reject null hypothesis: yes or no).
  • Conclusion on whether the underlying distribution of the control group is the same as the experimental one (Distributions are same: yes or no).

Your program must follow the format shown in the Example section. Choose the level of significance of \(0.05\). Note that there are only two options: p-value > 0.05 or p-value <= 0.05.

To conduct the test try out the scipy.stats.mannwhitneyu() function from the SciPy module.
